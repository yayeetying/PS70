Fusion 360 and Laser Cutting!

First, the box!

Aight, so let me be so real: the first iteration of the box I laser cut was WAYY too tiny. So after I went and changed the parameters of the length, height, width, etc. I imported the file to Rhino and realized that I actually made it too big. No biggie, I thought! I'll just adjust the length and height of the box manually in Rhino -- so I changed the vertical height and horizontal space in separate motions to make it fit within Rhino's constraints. Fatal mistake. I forgot that changing the dimensions in 2 swoops does not guarantee the previous ratio dimensions are kept. The result was this box with fingers that didn't quite fit into each other.



My third iteration, I made sure to pay attention to the dimensions of the box. When I imported that same file to Rhino, I rotated the box so that the long side of the box was taking the long side of the laser cutter's dimension. Here's my final product!
